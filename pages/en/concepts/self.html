🔮 Self (Atman)

Your True Nature - Pure Consciousness Beyond Body and Mind

What is the Self?

In the teachings of Sri Ramana Maharshi, the Self (Atman) is your true nature - the pure consciousness that witnesses all experiences but is never touched by them.

The Self is:

  • Eternal - never born, never dies
  • Unchanging - the permanent witness of all changing phenomena
  • Beyond body and mind - not the physical body or the thinking mind
  • Pure awareness - the "I am" consciousness that simply IS
  • Non-dual - one without a second (Advaita)

The Self vs. The Ego

Self (Atman) Ego (Ahamkara)
✅ Eternal, unchanging ❌ Temporary, constantly changing
✅ Witness consciousness ❌ Identifies with body-mind
✅ "I am" - pure being ❌ "I am this body/name"
✅ Ever-present, self-luminous ❌ Appears and disappears
✅ Free, blissful ❌ Bound, suffering

How to Realize the Self?

Ramana Maharshi taught that the Self is not something to be attained - it is what you already are. The problem is that we have forgotten our true nature due to ignorance (avidya).

The Direct Path: Self-Inquiry

Instead of trying to "get" the Self, turn your attention inward to discover what is already there:

"The Self is not somewhere far away. It is closer than the closest. It is your own Self - the 'I' that is always present."

The method is simple but profound:

  1. Notice that you exist - you are conscious
  2. This "I am" feeling is the Self
  3. Instead of looking outward at objects, turn attention backward to the source of "I"
  4. Abide there - this is the state of Self-abidance

Selected Quotes from Ramana Maharshi

"The Self is always the Self and there is no need to attain it. You are already the Self. The realization consists of getting rid of the wrong identification with the body and the mind."

"The Self is like the screen on which the cinema show is projected. The screen is real, the cinema show is merely a shadow play."

"Your own Self-realization is the greatest service you can render to the world."

"The Self is everywhere, all-pervading. But due to the veil of ignorance, you think it is limited to your body. Remove the veil, and you will see that you are everywhere."

Common Misunderstandings

❌ Misunderstanding: "I need to achieve Self-realization"

Truth: You don't need to achieve the Self - you ARE the Self. You only need to remove the wrong identification with the body-mind.

❌ Misunderstanding: "The Self is a special experience"

Truth: The Self is not an experience (which comes and goes). It is the witness of all experiences - the permanent "I am" consciousness.

❌ Misunderstanding: "I can see the Self as an object"

Truth: The Self cannot be objectified. It is the subject - the seer that cannot be seen, the knower that cannot be known.

The Self in Daily Life

Ramana Maharshi emphasized that Self-realization is not about escaping from daily life, but about living from the awareness of the Self while performing actions.

"The sage acts without the sense of being the doer. He performs actions like a king who issues commands without personally lifting a finger, or like a man dreaming that he is performing actions while remaining asleep."

This state is called Sahaja Samadhi - the natural state of effortless abidance in the Self while engaged in worldly activities.

Related Terms

  • Atman (Sanskrit) - The Self, the soul
  • Brahman - The Absolute, the totality of existence
  • Consciousness (Chit) - Pure awareness
  • Bliss (Ananda) - The natural state of the Self
  • Being (Sat) - Existence itself

In Advaita Vedanta, Atman (individual Self) = Brahman (universal Self). They are not two different things - there is only one consciousness appearing as many.
