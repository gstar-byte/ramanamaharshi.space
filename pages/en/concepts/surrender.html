🕉️ Surrender (Saranagati)

Offering the Ego to the Divine - A Direct Path to the Self

What is Surrender?

In Ramana Maharshi's teachings, surrender (Saranagati) means offering the ego completely to the Divine. It is not a practice of resignation, but a conscious surrender of the "I"-thought to its source.

Surrender is:

  • Offering the ego - Giving up the sense of doership
  • Total dependence on the Divine - "Not my will, but Thine"
  • A direct path - Can lead to the same goal as Self-Inquiry
  • Not fatalism - It is an active offering, not passive resignation
  • Complementary to Self-Inquiry - Both lead to the same goal

The Nature of True Surrender

🔹 Surrender is Not Resignation

Ramana Maharshi clarified that surrender is NOT:

  • Becoming passive or helpless
  • Believing "everything is God's will" and doing nothing
  • Escaping from responsibility

True surrender is an active offering of the ego. It is saying: "I am not the doer. You are the Doer."

🔹 The Five Aspects of Surrender (Prapatti)

  1. Anukulya-sankalpa - Accepting what is favorable
  2. Pratikulya-varjanam - Rejecting what is unfavorable
  3. Rakshishyati iti visvasah - Faith that the Divine will protect
  4. Mahyam nanyatha iti bhava - Self-surrender, no other refuge
  5. Karpanya - Humility, recognizing one's helplessness

How to Surrender?

Ramana Maharshi taught that surrender can be practiced in two ways:

Method 1: Gradual Surrender

  1. Offer actions - "I am not the doer; You are the Doer."
  2. Offer fruits - "The results are Yours, not mine."
  3. Offer thoughts - "All thoughts arise to You and subside in You."
  4. Offer the "I"-thought - "I am Yours."

Method 2: Total Surrender (Atma-Nivedana)

"Give yourself up to the Divine. The ego will die, and the true Self will shine forth. This is the highest form of surrender."

In this method, you simply say: "I am Yours. You take care of me. You are the Doer; I am not."

Selected Quotes

"Surrender is giving up the sense of 'I am the doer.' When you surrender, the ego subsides, and the Divine takes over. This is the same as Self-realization."

"Surrender can be practiced by anyone, anywhere. You don't need to go to a cave or forest. Just offer whatever you do to the Divine."

"The relationship between Self-Inquiry and surrender is like that between a bird's two wings. One wing is 'Who am I?' The other is 'I am Yours.' Both lead to the same goal."

"When surrender is complete, the ego is destroyed. The Divine then lives and acts through you. This is the state of liberation."

Surrender vs. Self-Inquiry

Surrender (Saranagati) Self-Inquiry (Atma Vichara)
✅ Path of devotion (Bhakti) ✅ Path of knowledge (Jnana)
✅ Offers ego to the Divine ✅ Traces ego to its source
✅ Relies on grace ✅ Direct investigation
✅ Suitable for emotional nature ✅ Suitable for intellectual nature
Both lead to the same goal - destruction of the ego

Practical Steps for Surrender

How to practice surrender in daily life:

✅ What to Do:

  • Before starting any action, say: "I am not the doer; You are the Doer."
  • When results come, say: "This is Your gift; I am not the enjoyer."
  • When difficulties arise, say: "This is Your will; I surrender to it."
  • When the ego rises, say: "I offer this 'I' to You. You take it."

❌ What NOT to Do:

  • Use surrender as an excuse for passivity
  • Believe "I have surrendered" while still identifying with the ego
  • Think surrender means "I don't need to make effort"
  • Separate surrender from Self-Inquiry

"Surrender is not a one-time event. It is a continuous offering. Every moment, offer the 'I'-thought to the Divine."

The Fate of the Ego After Surrender

After complete surrender, the ego is destroyed. But it may continue to function for the sake of the body, like a burnt rope that still looks like a rope but has no strength.

"In the state of surrender, the ego is like a deodar tree that has been cut at the root. It may appear to stand, but it will never sprout again."

This is the state of Sahaja Samadhi - natural abidance in the Self, where all actions are performed by the Divine, not the ego.
