✨ Grace (Prasada)

The Divine Power That Destroys the Ego and Reveals the Self

What is Grace?

In Ramana Maharshi's teachings, grace (Prasada) is the divine power that silently destroys the ego and reveals the true Self. It is not a reward for good behavior, but the natural outpouring of the Divine.

Grace is:

  • Unmerited - Not earned by effort or good deeds
  • Spontaneous - Arises from the Divine's compassion
  • Transformative - Destroys the ego and reveals the Self
  • Always available - Waiting for the ego to surrender
  • The source of all progress - Even Self-Inquiry is powered by grace

The Nature of Grace

🔹 Grace is Like the Sun

Ramana Maharshi used the analogy of the sun:

"Grace is like the sun. It is always shining. But you must remove the clouds (ego) to see it. The sun doesn't choose who to shine upon - it shines for all."

🔹 Grace Works in Two Ways

  1. Drawing you to the path - You feel attracted to Ramana Maharshi or his teachings
  2. Sustaining you on the path - You make progress despite obstacles
  3. Completing the journey - The ego is destroyed, and the Self shines forth

All three are grace. Without grace, you cannot even begin the spiritual journey.

How to Receive Grace?

Ramana Maharshi taught that grace cannot be demanded or earned. It flows naturally when the ego is surrendered.

The Key: Surrender

  1. Surrender the ego - Offer the "I"-thought to the Divine
  2. Be receptive - Don't resist grace's workings
  3. Trust - Know that the Divine is always with you
  4. Be patient - Grace works in its own time, not yours

"Grace is not something that comes from outside. It is your own true nature (the Self) revealing itself. When the ego subsides, grace shines forth."

Selected Quotes

"Grace is not a reward for good behavior. It is the natural outpouring of the Divine. Even the desire to seek God is due to grace."

"You think you are the doer. But in truth, it is grace that makes you do everything. Even your spiritual practice is powered by grace."

"Grace is like the mother's love for the child. It is unconditional. The mother doesn't wait for the child to be perfect - she loves the child as it is."

"When the ego is surrendered, grace rushes in like water into a hollow. The hollow (ego) must be empty for grace to fill it."

Grace vs. Self-Effort

Grace (Prasada) Self-Effort (Purushartha)
✅ Divine power ✅ Human effort
✅ Unearned, unmerited ✅ Required, but not the ultimate cause
✅ Sustains effort ✅ Prepares the mind
✅ Completes the journey ✅ Leads to surrender
Both are needed - effort leads to grace, grace completes the work

Ramana Maharshi said: "Effort (Purushartha) and grace (Prasada) are like the two wings of a bird. Both are needed to fly."

Practical Guidance

How to live in awareness of grace:

✅ What to Do:

  • Surrender daily - Offer the ego to the Divine
  • Be grateful - Recognize grace in every moment
  • Don't force - Allow grace to work in its own time
  • Trust - Know that you are being guided

❌ What NOT to Do:

  • Think you can earn grace through good deeds
  • Demand grace on your own timeline
  • Attribute progress solely to your own effort
  • Become passive, thinking "grace will do everything"

"The best way to receive grace is to surrender the ego. When the ego dies, grace fills the vacuum."

The Signs of Grace

How to know that grace is working in your life:

  1. Attraction to the Divine - You feel drawn to Ramana Maharshi or his teachings
  2. Dispassion (Vairagya) - Worldly pleasures lose their charm
  3. Yearning for the Self - A deep inner hunger to know "Who am I?"
  4. Peace in adversity - You remain calm despite difficulties
  5. Self-Inquiry arises spontaneously - You find yourself asking "Who am I?" without effort

These are all signs that grace is silently working to destroy the ego.

Grace After Self-Realization

After Self-realization, grace continues to flow. But now it is no longer "received" - you ARE grace.

"In the realized being, there is no difference between the individual and grace. They have become one. Grace flows through them spontaneously."

This is the state of Jivanmukti - liberation while alive. The body may continue to act, but it is grace that acts, not the ego.
