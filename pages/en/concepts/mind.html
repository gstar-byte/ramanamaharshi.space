🧠 Mind (Manas)

The Instrument of Thought That Must Be Transcended

What is the Mind?

In Ramana Maharshi's teachings, the mind (Manas) is the faculty of thinking, feeling, and willing. It is the instrument through which the Self appears to be limited and individual.

The mind is:

  • Not the Self - It is an instrument, not the knower
  • The cause of bondage - When identified with, it causes suffering
  • The seat of thoughts - All thoughts arise in the mind
  • To be transcended - Must be dissolved to realize the Self
  • Identical with the ego - "Mind" and "ego" are often used interchangeably

The Nature of the Mind

🔹 The Mind is Like a Mirror

Ramana Maharshi used the analogy of a mirror:

"The mind is like a mirror that reflects consciousness. When the mirror is clean, it reflects the Self clearly. When it is dirty (with thoughts), the reflection is distorted."

🔹 The Mind's Two Functions

  1. Sankalpa - Formation of thoughts and desires
  2. Vikalpa - Imagination and mental projection

When these two functions subside, the mind becomes still and turns inward.

How to Transcend the Mind

Ramana Maharshi taught that the mind cannot be destroyed by force. It can only be transcended by turning it inward through Self-Inquiry.

The Process:

  1. Don't follow thoughts outward - When thoughts arise, don't pursue them
  2. Turn the mind inward - Ask "To whom do these thoughts arise?"
  3. Trace to the source - Follow the "I"-thought back to its origin
  4. Abide in the Self - The mind will subside like a wave into the ocean

"The mind is like a tothless tiger. It appears formidable but has no real power. When you turn inward and ignore it, it will subside."

Selected Quotes

"The mind is only a collection of thoughts. The 'I'-thought is the root of all other thoughts. If you trace the 'I'-thought to its source, the mind will subside."

"The mind is like a boy who pretends to be a tiger to frighten others. When you shine the light of awareness on it, it will run away."

"When the mind is outward-turned, it is called the ego. When it is inward-turned, it is called the Self. The mind is actually non-different from the Self."

"The mind is like a piece of cotton - light and easily blown away. When you practice Self-Inquiry, the wind of wisdom will blow it away."

Mind vs. Self

Mind (Manas) Self (Atman)
❌ Changing, fluctuating ✅ Unchanging, permanent
❌ Instrument of thought ✅ Witness of thought
❌ Bound by karma ✅ Free, beyond karma
❌ Suffering ✅ Bliss (Ananda)
❌ Limited, individual ✅ Infinite, universal

The States of Mind

🔹 Three States of Consciousness:

  1. Waking state (Jagrat) - Mind extroverted, engaged with the world
  2. Dream state (Svapna) - Mind creates its own world
  3. Deep sleep (Sushupti) - Mind subsided, but ignorance remains
  4. Turiya (The Fourth) - Mind transcended, Self realized

Ramana Maharshi taught that beyond these three states is Turiya - the fourth state where the mind is transcended and the Self shines forth.

Practical Guidance

How to deal with the mind in daily life:

✅ What to Do:

  • Ignore the mind's chatter - Don't identify with thoughts
  • Practice Self-Inquiry - Turn the mind inward
  • Be the witness - Watch thoughts without judging
  • Surrender - Offer the mind to the Divine

❌ What NOT to Do:

  • Try to "kill" the mind by force
  • Follow every thought that arises
  • Believe the mind's judgments and fears
  • Try to control the mind through willpower alone

"The mind is like a wild horse. You cannot tame it by pulling the reins harder. You must gently turn it inward through Self-Inquiry."

The Fate of the Mind After Self-Realization

After Self-realization, the mind may continue to function for the sake of the body, but it is like a burnt rope - it has no strength to bind.

"In the realized being, the mind is like a deodar tree that has been cut at the root. It may appear to stand, but it will never sprout again."

This is the state of Sahaja Samadhi - the natural state where the mind is transcended but continues to function without egoic identification.
