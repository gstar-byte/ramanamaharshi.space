🛤️ Practice Methods

Core practical methods taught by Sri Ramana · The path to Self-Realization

💎 Core Methods

🔍 Self-Inquiry (Atma-Vichara / "Who am I?")

The most central and direct teaching of Sri Ramana. When thoughts arise, do not follow them, but ask: "Who am I?" Do not look for a mental answer, but dive into the feeling of "I". Whenever other thoughts appear, let them go and return to the inquiry.

Practical Steps:

  1. Sit in a quiet place
  2. Let the mind settle
  3. Ask: "Who am I?"
  4. When other thoughts arise, gently let them go and return to the question
  5. Dive into the sense of "I", seeking no verbal answer
  6. Practice continuously to lead the mind back to its source

Ramana said: "Self-Inquiry is the only method to remove ignorance."

🙏 Surrender (Saranagati)

Complete surrender to the will of the Divine or the Guru. Surrender means letting go of the illusion of being the "doer," recognizing that all actions belong to the Self or God. You may still act, but without attachment to results.

Objects of Surrender:

  • God / Universal Will
  • The Guru
  • The Self / Heart

Ramana said: "Surrender is not giving up action, but giving up the false identity of the doer."

🔮 Repetition (Japa / Mantra)

The repetition of a sacred name or mantra. Japa helps steady a restless mind and prepares it for deeper inquiry. Maintain focus while chanting, allowing the mantra to penetrate deep into consciousness.

Recommended Mantras:

  • Om
  • So'ham (I am He / I am Brahman)
  • Sri Ram
  • Any divine name you have faith in

Repetition eventually dissolves naturally into silence.

🤫 Silence (Mauna)

Sri Ramana's most signature teaching was through Silence. His silence was not just the absence of speech, but a deep state of being. In silence, the teaching is transmitted through "presence" rather than words.

Two levels of Silence:

  • Outer Silence: Maintaining speechlessness and quietude
  • Inner Silence: The cessation of mind, beyond thoughts

Ramana remained almost entirely in silence for the last 52 years of his life.

🧘 Meditation (Dhyana)

Fixing the mind on a single point. Meditation is the foundation of all spiritual practice, and can be done through focus on breath, mantras, or inquiry.

Points to remember:

  • Maintain a steady posture with a straight spine
  • Do not seek special experiences or states
  • Samadhi is not the goal; realizing the Self is

Meditation eventually dissolves into "non-meditation"—natural abiding in the Self.

🔧 Auxiliary Methods

🙏 Devotion (Bhakti)

Approaching the Divine through love and devotion. The path of devotion and the path of inquiry complement each other and eventually lead to the same point.

📖 Scripture Study (Sravana)

Reading the Upanishads, Vedantic classics, and Sri Ramana's works. Remember: the Self cannot be found in books; it must eventually be self-realized.

🏔️ Pilgrimage

Visiting holy places, especially Mount Arunachala and the Ashram. Holy places have a special energy field that aids spiritual progress.

🧘 Focus on the Heart (Hridayam)

Placing attention on the 'I' in the Heart center. Sri Ramana said the Heart is on the right side of the chest. Returning to the Heart is returning to the Self.

💬 Practice Related Q&A

Q: How exactly should I perform Self-Inquiry?

A: Let your mind be quiet. Then ask: "Who am I?" Do not look for a verbal answer. Instead, dive into the feeling of "I". As you continue to do this, the mind will return to its source—the Self.
— Be As You Are
Q: How long does it take to attain realization?

A: It is not a matter of time. You must ask yourself: Am I ready to let go? Let go of attachment to the world, let go of the concept of "me." Once you truly let go, realization happens instantly.
— Sri Ramana Maharshi
Q: What is the best method of practice?

A: There is no "best" method. Each seeker should choose the method that suits their temperament. But ultimately, all methods point to the same goal—knowing the Self.
— Sri Ramana Maharshi
