👩 Venkataramana Nanda

Venkataramana (1873–1951)

Lifelong devotee and guardian of Sri Ramanasramam.

👩

Born: 1873

Mahanirvana: 1951, Arunachala

Identity: Aunt of Sri Ramana Maharshi

Role: Caretaker of the Maharshi and Ashram administrator

🌟 Spiritual Bond with the Sage

Venkataramana Nanda was Maharshi's aunt and remained by his side from the very moment of his awakening at age 16. When the young Venkataraman experienced his mystical death-experience, his family was at a loss; it was his aunt who encouraged him to pursue his spiritual calling.

In 1896, when Maharshi decided to leave Madurai for the sacred hill of Arunachala, Nanda secretly followed him to ensure his wellbeing during the journey and cared for his needs in the early years of his arrival.

🏔️ Guardian of Arunachala

In 1922, as Sri Ramanasramam was formally established around the mother's shrine, Nanda became a pivotal figure in the Ashram's administration. She took charge of Maharshi's daily personal care, ensuring he could focus entirely on instructing the seekers who flocked to him.

Though Maharshi remained largely in silence for 52 years, his presence was the most powerful teaching. Nanda played a vital role in the Ashram's daily operations, facilitating the transmission of his grace to the world.

🙏 Continuing the Legacy

Following Maharshi's Mahanirvana in 1950, Nanda continued to uphold the traditions of the Ashram. She witnessed the influx of visitors and scholars from across the globe, dedicated to preserving the authenticity of the Maharshi's legacy.

A year later, in 1951, Nanda passed away at Arunachala, concluding a 55-year journey of unwavering service to the Sage.

💎 Reflections

"He does not speak, but his presence itself is the teaching. He needs no words—his stillness is the most profound instruction."
— Venkataramana Nanda on the Maharshi
