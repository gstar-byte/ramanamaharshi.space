📝 David Godman

David Godman (1953–)

The foremost contemporary biographer and chronicler of Sri Ramana Maharshi's life and teachings.

📝

Born: 1953, Stoke-on-Trent, United Kingdom

Education: Oxford University (entered 1972)

Role: Author, Editor, Translator, Filmmaker

At the Ashram: Librarian at Sri Ramanasramam (1978–1985)

Major Works: Be As You Are, Nothing Ever Happened

Residency: Resident of Tiruvannamalai since 1976

🌍 Early Life and Awakening

David Godman was born in 1953. His father was a headmaster and his mother a physiotherapist. In 1972, he entered Oxford University.

During his second year at Oxford, he happened upon a book by Arthur Osborne about Sri Ramana Maharshi. This reading completely transformed his trajectory:

"It wasn't that I had found a new set of ideas I believed in. It was more an experience — I was pulled into a state of silence. In that space of silence I understood directly and intuitively what Ramana's words were hinting at and pointing to. Because that state was itself the answer to all my questions... the interest in looking for solutions elsewhere just evaporated."
— David Godman

Since then, Maharshi's teachings have been the central focus of Godman's life and work.

🏔️ The Arunachala Years

1976 — David first visited Sri Ramanasramam in Tiruvannamalai, beginning a lifelong association with the sacred hill.

1978–1985 — He served as the librarian at Sri Ramanasramam for eight years. This period allowed him to deeply research original manuscripts, disciples' memoirs, and classical Tamil and Sanskrit texts.

During the late 1970s, he also frequently traveled to Mumbai to attend the satsangs of Nisargadatta Maharaj, the sage of I Am That fame, who also profoundly influenced his understanding.

In the early 1980s, he began visiting Lakshmana Swamy, a direct disciple who realized the Self in Maharshi's presence in 1949. At Swamy's request, David wrote No Mind – I am the Self.

📚 Literary Career

Over nearly 40 years, David has written, edited, or translated 16 books regarding Maharshi and his lineage. Key milestones include:

1985 — Publication of Be As You Are by Penguin. This thematic collection of Maharshi's dialogues remains the most popular introductory text for readers worldwide.

1993–1997 — At the invitation of H.W.L. Poonja (Papaji), David moved to Lucknow. He produced a documentary on Papaji and edited the massive three-volume biography Nothing Ever Happened.

2000–2002 — Published The Power of the Presence (3 volumes), a collection of first-person accounts by disciples who lived with Maharshi between 1896 and 1950.

2004–2008 — Collaborated with T.V. Venkatasubramanian and Robert Butler to translate the profound Tamil verses of Muruganar, including the authoritative Guru Vachaka Kovai (The Garland of Guru's Sayings).
